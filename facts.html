1.5°C
The critical global temperature threshold we must not exceed to avoid catastrophic climate effects.
Current trajectories put us at 2.4–2.7°C by 2100 without urgent policy changes. Every fraction of a degree matters — a 2°C world sees 99% of coral reefs destroyed and 164 million people facing severe water scarcity. The Paris Agreement's 1.5°C target requires halving emissions by 2030.
IPCC Sixth Assessment Report, 2023
8M+
Metric tons of plastic enter our oceans every single year — one garbage truck every minute.
By 2050, there could be more plastic in the ocean than fish by weight. Only 9% of all plastic ever produced has been recycled. Microplastics have been found in human blood, breast milk, and the deepest ocean trenches. EcoQuest's ocean missions are directly inspired by this crisis.
UNEP Global Plastics Report, 2023
3.5B
People depend on ocean biodiversity for their primary protein source, making marine health a food security issue.
33% of global fish stocks are overfished, and half are at maximum sustainable yield. Ocean acidification has increased 26% since industrialisation — threatening the shellfish and coral ecosystems that underpin ocean food chains.
FAO State of World Fisheries, 2024
15B
Trees are cut down every year globally — 10 million hectares of forest lost annually.
Forests absorb about 2.6 billion tonnes of CO₂ per year. Reforestation is one of the most cost-effective climate solutions — planting one trillion trees could capture two-thirds of all the CO₂ humans have emitted. EcoQuest's Forest Mission teaches why every tree matters.
Global Forest Watch, 2024
68%
Average decline in wildlife populations since 1970, according to the Living Planet Report.
We are in Earth's sixth mass extinction event. The rate of species loss is 100–1,000 times higher than the natural background rate. Once a species is lost, the ecological functions it performed — pollination, seed dispersal, pest control — are lost permanently.
WWF Living Planet Report, 2024
420ppm
CO₂ concentration in the atmosphere today — the highest level in over 3 million years of Earth's history.
Pre-industrial levels were 280ppm. We crossed the 400ppm threshold in 2013 and have not dipped below it since. The last time CO₂ was this high, sea levels were 20 metres higher and forests grew at the poles.
NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory, 2024

$10T
The projected size of the global green economy by 2030, across clean energy, sustainable agriculture, and circular economy sectors.
The transition to net-zero could create 65 million new green jobs by 2030 while displacing 11 million — a significant net gain that demands rapid reskilling. Children learning green finance today are being prepared for the most in-demand careers of the 2030s.
IRENA / World Economic Forum, 2024
$7.5T
Global ESG assets under management — tripling from 2017 to 2024, reshaping capital markets worldwide.
Over 85% of institutional investors now incorporate ESG factors. By 2025, ESG assets are projected to constitute one-third of all global AUM. Understanding ESG is no longer a niche skill — it is becoming fundamental to finance careers.
Bloomberg Intelligence, 2024
$841B
Total global investment in renewable energy in 2023 — the highest ever recorded, surpassing fossil fuel investment for the first time.
Solar alone attracted $380B. The cost of solar electricity has fallen 89% in a decade, making it the cheapest electricity in history. Wind, batteries, and green hydrogen are following the same trajectory — creating enormous employment demand.
IEA World Energy Investment, 2024
170+
Countries now have a carbon pricing mechanism covering 23% of global greenhouse gas emissions.
The global carbon market grew to $900 billion in 2023. Understanding carbon economics — credits, offsets, cap-and-trade — is one of the most valuable financial skills a young person can develop. GreenMoney teaches this through direct, consequence-based simulation.
World Bank Carbon Pricing Dashboard, 2024
24M
New green economy jobs projected for India alone by 2030 across renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, and green construction.
Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing region for green employment. Skills in environmental science, sustainable finance, and green engineering are in critical shortage. The students playing GreenMoney today are the workforce of 2035.
ILO Green Jobs Outlook India, 2023
82%
Of Gen Z investors say they want their portfolio to reflect their environmental and social values — reshaping capital markets.
This demographic will inherit $68 trillion in wealth over the next decade. Their investment preferences will fundamentally reshape which industries survive. Companies ignoring ESG are increasingly finding it harder to attract both talent and capital.
Morgan Stanley Sustainable Signals, 2023
